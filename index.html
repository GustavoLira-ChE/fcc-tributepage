Porfirio Diaz

33rd President of Mexico

Some important dates on Diaz's Life

  • 1830: José de la Cruz Porfirio Diaz Mori was born in Oaxaca City, Mexico.

  • 1846: Diaz joined with seminary students who volunteered as soldiers to repel the U.S. invasion during Mexican-American War.

  • 1855: Diaz joined a band of liberal guerrillas who were fighting Santa Anna's government.

  • 1865: Participated in Mexico's great victory over the Franch when they first invaded, Diaz had advanced to the rank of general and was placed in command of an infantry brigade.

  • 1870: Ran against President Júarez and Vice President Sebastián Lerdo de Tejada.

  • 1876: Occupied Mexico City, and Lerdo left Mexico for exile in New York. Diaz did not take formal control of the presidency until the beginning of 1877.

  • 1880: Diaz stepped down from the presidency, with his ally, General Manuel González, one of the trustworthy members of his political network, elected president in a fully constitutional manner.

  • 1884: Despite Diaz's previous protestations of "no re-election", he ran for a second term.

  • 1911: Porfirio went into exile.

  • 1915: Diaz died in exile in Paris, France.
